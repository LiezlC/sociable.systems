THE PREMISE: Global development and impact evaluators are rushing to feed qualitative field data, community interviews, and M&E reports into commercial AI. Without an operational governance framework, this doesn't improve insight; it just accelerates the erasure of nuance and outsources moral judgment to a statistical probability engine.

CORE MODULES

When AI-generated summaries are wrong, junior staff often end up carrying responsibility for outputs they did not actually verify.

Junior researchers and field teams are increasingly using AI to synthesize messy field notes into clean summaries. This module teaches teams how to recognize when they have stopped being analysts and started being "liability sponges"—signing their names to aggregated narratives created by an algorithm.

DELIVERABLE: A structural audit of your team's current data-processing pipeline to identify where human authority has been quietly replaced by automated consensus. Establishment of localized "Stop Work Authority".

Because commercial AI is trained to be polite and agreeable, it inherently dilutes the severity of community grievances. We map exactly who pays the price for that sanitized data.

Community grievances and social realities are rarely friction-free. This module introduces the "Victim Register" mechanism: training teams to stress-test their own reports by shifting the vantage point to the person downstream who absorbs the cost of the smoothed, sanitized abstraction.

DELIVERABLE: A framework for establishing "Stop Work Authority" when a model's translation of community pain becomes too abstract to be operational.

If you cannot legally look under the hood of your vendor's algorithm, you are not doing evaluation—you are taking dictation. We rewrite your procurement rules to fix this.

Before you deploy a tool to evaluate a vulnerable population, you must be able to audit how that tool decides what matters. This module covers the "Calvin Convention" for M&E procurement: non-negotiable clauses that guarantee your right to interrogate the model, demand evidence access, and halt processing.

DELIVERABLE: Vendor Interrogation Scripts designed to break through safety theater and establish who holds the liability when the system gets it wrong.
